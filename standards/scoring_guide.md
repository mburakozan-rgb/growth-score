# Scoring Guide: Eight Pillars of Growth Capabilities in the AI Era

This scoring guide defines the benchmarks and evaluation criteria for each of the eight pillars of growth capabilities in the AI age. The agent must evaluate the target company against these criteria, assigning a maturity score between 1.0 (Nascent/Traditional) and 10.0 (State-of-the-Art AI-Integrated).

---

## Foundation Layer: Core Growth Capabilities

### Pillar 1: Product & Customer Experience Excellence
*   **Definition**: The core product serves as the habit-forming foundation that everything else amplifies. Winning in the AI era requires products driven by a clear brand purpose that inspires customer advocacy, high engagement, and organic retention.
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: No clear organic viral loops or habit-forming mechanics; low customer retention; brand purpose is generic or disconnected from the product experience; weak online ratings (app store/reviews).
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Moderate product habituation; clear brand purpose that resonates with some users; average retention and NPS; product relies heavily on paid acquisition rather than organic advocacy.
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Extremely strong organic retention and advocacy loops; high app store ratings (>4.5) with rapid update cycles; brand purpose is deeply integrated into a frictionless UX that creates immediate user habits.

### Pillar 2: Content Strategy & Operating System (SEO to AEO)
*   **Definition**: Optimization of content for Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) and multimodal discovery (text, voice, visual) in a global context, ensuring search crawlers and AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) easily discover and cite the brand.
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: Traditional SEO approach focusing solely on keywords; blocks AI crawlers in `robots.txt` without a strategic reason; missing structured data (schema.org); no voice or visual optimization.
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Adapting to AEO; allows some AI crawlers; includes basic Schema markup; produces high-quality content but lack global multimodal formats (voice/visual indexing).
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Full AEO optimization; schema-marked video, image, and FAQ content; highly cited as a source in AI searches; strategic crawl permissions (allowing LLM crawlers for visibility while protecting core IP); seamless multi-regional content operations.

### Pillar 3: Advanced Segmentation & Psychology-Based Personalization
*   **Definition**: Moving beyond static demographics to understand emotional contexts and situational needs (e.g. user behavior at 9 AM Monday vs. 8 PM Friday).
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: Basic list-based or demographic segmentation (age, gender); static email blasts; no real-time website personalization.
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Dynamic behavioral segmentation (e.g., cart abandoners, frequency of visit); uses rules-based personalization engines; uses basic telemetry tools.
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Real-time psychological and situational personalization (adapting UI/content based on current mood, context, or situational trigger); uses advanced CDP (Customer Data Platform) like Segment or Tealium with dynamic yield engines.

### Pillar 4: The Experimentation & Growth Culture
*   **Definition**: Growth viewed as a company-wide responsibility. Measuring success by velocity of learning and experimentation frequency, backed by technical excellence.
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: Zero or very few A/B tests run per year; growth is isolated to a siloed marketing team; culture is risk-averse; slow release cycles.
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Regular A/B testing on landing pages; dedicated growth team; toolchains like Optimizely or VWO are integrated; learning is shared occasionally.
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Continuous experimentation culture running hundreds of tests simultaneously; feature flags (LaunchDarkly/Split) fully integrated; growth engineering teams; high learning velocity celebrated as a core metric.

## Orchestration Layer: Advanced Growth Capabilities

### Pillar 5: Growth Loops & Advanced Journey Orchestration
*   **Definition**: Weaving personalized experiences into systematic customer journeys that build compounding value (referrals, viral loops, usage-based loops).
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: Linear acquisition funnels (Ad -> Landing Page -> Purchase) with high customer acquisition cost (CAC); no natural referral loops or product-led triggers.
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Basic email onboarding flows; referral incentives exist but are tacked-on rather than embedded in the product; linear retention sequences.
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Sophisticated compounding growth loops (content loops, referral loops, data loops); dynamic multi-channel onboarding journeys that react instantly to user behavior; low CAC due to high organic loop mechanics.

### Pillar 6: Distribution Strategy & Channel Orchestration
*   **Definition**: Ensuring optimized journeys reach customers across all touchpoints: traditional digital channels, emerging media, and integrations with external AI agents.
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: Relies on one or two channels (e.g., Meta ads, standard search); siloed channel execution; no AI assistant support.
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Omni-channel marketing (social, email, search) with basic coordination; basic API integration; voice assistant support planned.
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Advanced API-first distribution allowing external AI agents, chatbots, and plugins to interact directly with the brand; presence on emerging platforms; dynamic cross-channel orchestration.

---

## Intelligence Layer: Strategic Growth Capabilities

### Pillar 7: Measurement & Attribution
*   **Definition**: Understanding performance not just individually, but as an interconnected, multi-touch system, utilizing server-side tracking and privacy-safe frameworks.
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: Simple last-click attribution; relies on standard client-side browser cookies (subject to adblockers); no server-side conversion tracking.
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Multi-touch attribution (MTA) models; client-side Google Tag Manager; basic server-side API setups (e.g., Meta Conversions API).
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Full server-side tag management (e.g., server-side GTM, Segment); first-party data collection; advanced marketing mix modeling (MMM) or custom data warehouse multi-touch attribution.

### Pillar 8: Tools & Platforms
*   **Definition**: The APIs, analytics infrastructure, and orchestration engines enabling all other pillars to function together as an intelligent, self-optimizing system.
*   **Scoring Rubric**:
    *   **1.0 – 3.0 (Nascent)**: Fragmented tech stack with siloed data; custom scripts that break frequently; no unified customer view.
    *   **4.0 – 7.0 (Developing)**: Integrated stack using standard mid-market SaaS tools; unified data via a standard CRM; APIs available for internal use.
    *   **8.0 – 10.0 (Exemplary)**: Cloud-native data stack (Snowflake, BigQuery) with centralized Customer Data Platform (CDP); fully documented public API ecosystem; real-time event pipelines supporting human interfaces and AI agents alike.
